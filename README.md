## Project Overview 
This repository features a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) conducted on an e-commerce transactional dataset containing 1,200 orders and 3,535 total units sold. Utilizing Microsoft Excel, Power Query, and advanced Pivot Table modeling, this project digs beneath the surface numbers to uncover core operational trends, seasonal demand shifts, customer payment habits, and localized fulfillment challenges. The ultimate objective was to transform raw behavioral logs into high-impact, actionable business intelligence capable of optimizing supply chains and boosting retention metrics.

## Core Analytical Methodologies 
To extract objective business narratives from the dataset, the analysis was broken down into several distinct phases: 
1. Data Hygiene & Power Query ETL: Structured and cleaned the source files, verified data types, and isolated variable streams. 
2. Descriptive Statistical Modeling: Implemented basic and advanced statistical profiles to capture data distribution baselines (Mean, Median, Skewness, Kurtosis).
3. Behavioral & Revenue Segmentation: Grouped transaction patterns across dimensions such as structural product tiers, temporal parameters, promotional usage, and payment configurations. 

## Technical Visualizations & Data Modelling 
## 1. Key Observation Summary (Pivot Tables) A multi-dimensional view mapping order distribution across months, status variations, product performance metrics, and preferred payment gateways.

### 2. Descriptive Statistics (Basic Approach) Establishing baseline metrics including sums, counts, averages, and positional midpoints across structural numerical values. 

### 3. Advanced Statistical Profiling Deep-dive distribution mechanics exploring sample variances, standard errors, structural skewness, and confidence coefficients.

## Strategic Business Insights 
### Financial & Volume Baselines 
1. Total Revenue Portfolio: Generated $1.26M, gross volume across 1,200 independent orders with 3,535 items changing hands. 
2. Core Revenue Engines: Chairs, Printers, and Laptops emerged as heavy hitters, dominating both absolute financial yield and transactional volume. 
3. Transactional Velocity: Printers stood out as the most consistently ordered individual asset line item, pointing to a rigid, highly predictable market demand loop. 

### Temporal Performance & Campaign Traction 

1. Seasonality Constraints: Peak transaction velocities compressed tightly into June, followed by a sustained, steady tapering across Q3 and Q4. This indicates a high probability of structural summer market seasonality. 
2. Incentive Engineering: The "FREESHIP" promotional script dramatically outpaced fixed-rate markdown codes ("SAVE10", "WINTER15"), acting as the primary conversion catalyst for high-volume baskets. 
3. Gateway Ecosystems: Digital transactions split clean down the middle, with Credit Cards and Online Wallets securing the highest absolute revenue share over physical cash alternatives. 

### Operational Vulnerabilities 
Fulfillment Fractures: Combined structural leakages from Cancelled and Returned processing reached a worrying 497 orders (~41% of total order volume). This represents a significant bottleneck threatening net profit margins and inflating warehouse restock loops.

## Strategic Recommendations 
1. Supply Chain Optimization: Align inventory buffers for Chairs, Printers, and Laptops to match the aggressive demand spikes seen in Q2, while safety stock targets can safely scale down heading into Q3.
2. Fulfillment & QA Audit: Initiate an immediate root-cause investigation into the logistics loop to diagnose the high 41% cancellation and return rate (e.g., investigating courier delays, product description accuracy, or sorting errors).
3. Refining Promotion Strategy: Phase out lower-performing fixed-value coupons and anchor future marketing budgets around dynamic free-shipping triggers to drive higher conversion rates.

## Key Technical Takeaways 
"This analysis proves that data analytics is far more than just plotting coordinates on charts. It requires building deep contextual narratives out of raw numbers, challenging systemic inefficiencies, and framing ambiguous behavioral footprints into crisp, executive-ready directives."
